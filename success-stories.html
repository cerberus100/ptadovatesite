Maria's Complete Journey

"I am a 54-year-old diabetic who works two jobs to support my family. When I developed a foot ulcer that wouldn't heal, I tried to treat it myself because I couldn't afford to see a doctor. By the time I finally went to the emergency room, the infection was so bad that doctors said I might lose my leg."

The Challenge

Maria came to us in desperation. She had been struggling with a diabetic foot ulcer for 6 months. Without insurance, she couldn't afford to see a specialist. The wound had become severely infected, and emergency room doctors were recommending amputation. She was quoted $50,000 for limb-saving treatments that insurance wouldn't cover.

Our Action Plan

Week 1: Emergency Response

  • Applied for hospital charity care (approved within 3 days)
  • Connected Maria with wound care specialist willing to work with charity care
  • Arranged emergency Medicaid application

Week 2: Financial Assistance

  • Secured $8,000 from Patient Advocate Foundation for co-pays
  • Applied to pharmaceutical programs for infection medications (approved for $6,000/year)
  • Arranged medical equipment loans for specialized wound care supplies

Month 1-3: Treatment & Support

  • Weekly wound care specialist visits covered by charity care
  • Advanced wound dressings through equipment assistance program
  • Ongoing case management and emotional support

The Results

  • Complete wound healing in 3 months
  • Leg amputation avoided
  • $18,000 in financial assistance secured
  • Ongoing diabetic care established
  • Returned to work full-time
  • Family's financial crisis resolved

Maria's Words Today

"Helping Hands didn't just save my leg - they saved my life and my family's future. I can't believe how quickly they found help that I didn't even know existed. Now I tell everyone I know about them. They turned my worst nightmare into a success story."

Follow-Up Care

Maria continues to receive support through our long-term case management program. We helped establish ongoing diabetic care to prevent future complications and connected her with diabetes education resources. She now serves as a peer mentor for other patients facing similar challenges.
