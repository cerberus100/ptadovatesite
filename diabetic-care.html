Common Diabetic Wound Types We Help With

Diabetic Foot Ulcers

The most common diabetic wound. Often develops on pressure points of feet due to neuropathy and poor circulation.

Risk: 15% of diabetics develop foot ulcers. 85% of amputations start with an ulcer.

ðŸ¦µ

Venous Leg Ulcers

Chronic wounds on lower legs caused by poor venous circulation, more common in diabetics with vascular disease.

Treatment: Requires specialized compression therapy and wound care.

Surgical Site Complications

Post-surgical wounds that heal poorly due to diabetes. Includes amputation sites, bypass surgeries, and other procedures.

Challenge: Higher infection rates and slower healing in diabetics.

Pressure Ulcers

Bedsores that develop from prolonged pressure, especially in diabetics with limited mobility or neuropathy.

Prevention: Position changes, pressure-relieving devices, blood sugar control.
