Emergency Financial Help

When you're facing an immediate medical emergency and can't afford treatment, these resources can provide rapid financial assistance within 24-48 hours.

Emergency Financial Assistance Programs

Emergency Medicaid

For: Life-threatening emergencies, regardless of immigration status

Coverage: Emergency room visits, emergency surgery, emergency dialysis

Apply: At the hospital during your emergency visit

Income limit: Varies by state, but covers very low-income individuals

Timeline: Can be applied retroactively for up to 3 months

Important: Ask hospital social worker to help you apply during your visit

Hospital Emergency Financial Assistance

For: Immediate financial crisis preventing treatment

Coverage: Emergency procedures, life-saving treatments

Apply: Speak to hospital case manager or social worker immediately

Decision time: Often same-day approval for genuine emergencies

What to say: "I have a medical emergency and cannot afford treatment. I need emergency financial assistance."

Your rights: Hospitals cannot refuse emergency care due to inability to pay

Emergency Medication Programs

For: Life-threatening medication needs

Coverage: Emergency supply of critical medications

Apply: Doctor calls pharmaceutical company emergency line

Timeline: Same-day or next-day approval possible

What's covered: Usually 30-day emergency supply while formal application processes

Ask your doctor: "Can you call for emergency medication assistance?"

Local Crisis Intervention Funds

For: Immediate financial crisis assistance

Coverage: Emergency medical bills, urgent medication costs

Apply: Contact local United Way, Salvation Army, or religious organizations

Typical amounts: $500-$2,500 for emergency needs

Timeline: 24-48 hours for approval

Call 211: They can connect you to local emergency assistance programs

What to Do Right Now

🏥 If You're Currently at the Hospital

  1. Ask to speak to a hospital social worker or case manager immediately
  2. Say: "I have a medical emergency and cannot afford treatment. I need emergency financial assistance."
  3. Ask about: Emergency Medicaid, hospital charity care, emergency payment plans
  4. Don't leave: It's easier to get help while you're still at the hospital
  5. Get contact information: Names and phone numbers of everyone who helps you

💊 If You Need Emergency Medication

  1. Call your doctor's office immediately - explain it's an emergency
  2. Ask your doctor to call the pharmaceutical company's emergency assistance line
  3. Go to the emergency room if the medication is life-threatening
  4. Call the drug manufacturer directly - most have 24/7 emergency lines
  5. Ask about samples: Doctor offices often have emergency medication samples

If You Have Immediate Medical Bills

  1. Call the billing department immediately - don't wait
  2. Explain your emergency situation - be honest about your financial crisis
  3. Ask for: Emergency payment plan, hardship discount, charity care application
  4. Request documentation: Get any agreements in writing
  5. Don't ignore bills: Communicate even if you can't pay

Emergency Documents to Gather

📄 Essential Documents for Emergency Assistance

Gather these quickly to speed up emergency applications:

Identity & Income:
  • Driver's license or ID
  • Social Security card
  • Most recent pay stub
  • Bank statement
Medical & Insurance:
  • Insurance cards (if any)
  • Medical bills
  • Prescription information
  • Doctor's contact information

Don't delay treatment to gather documents! Apply for assistance even if you don't have all paperwork ready.
