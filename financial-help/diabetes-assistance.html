Government Diabetes Assistance Programs

Medicare Extra Help (LIS)

Low Income Subsidy program that dramatically reduces prescription drug costs for Medicare beneficiaries.

Benefits for Diabetics:

  • Insulin co-pays as low as $0-$3.95
  • No coverage gap (donut hole)
  • Reduced premiums and deductibles
  • Covers diabetes testing supplies

üìû Apply: 1-800-MEDICARE

Income Limit (2024): $21,870 (single) / $29,580 (couple)

Auto-qualify if you have: Medicaid, SSI, MSP, or SNAP benefits

340B Covered Entity Pharmacies

Federally Qualified Health Centers offering discounted medications to uninsured and underinsured patients.

  • Insulin at manufacturer cost (50-90% off)
  • Sliding fee scale based on income
  • No insurance required
  • Diabetes education included

üîç Find Locations: findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov

üìû Info: 1-888-ASK-HRSA

Tip: Many FQHCs also offer wound care services and can coordinate your diabetes + wound care.

State Diabetes Assistance Programs

Many states offer diabetes-specific assistance programs for residents who don't qualify for federal programs.

Common State Programs:

  • Diabetes prevention programs
  • Free glucose meter programs
  • Diabetes education grants
